# cta-captions

**What is this?** Caption chunks for the ask — the closing frame. One clear action, coral highlight
on the CTA keyword (`kommentera`, `länken`, `testa`, `dm:a`).

**When to use it?** Over `cta-scene` (the closing coral-pill CTA plate). The caption reinforces the
spoken ask; it never competes with the on-plate button copy — it *echoes* it.

**What feeling?** Calm, direct, one ask. A senior operator telling you exactly what to do next, no
urgency theatre, no "link in bio!!!". Full-stop close.

---

Format per line: chunks `[…]`, coral highlight in **bold** (the CTA keyword).

1. `**kommentera**` · `så skickar jag` · `länken`
2. `skriv` · `**"reel"**` · `i kommentaren`
3. `**dm:a** mig` · `så visar jag`
4. `länken` · `finns i` · `**bion.**`
5. `vill du ha` · `mallen` · `**kommentera.**`
6. `**testa**` · `själv idag`
7. `följ` · `för fler` · `**så här.**`
8. `spara` · `den här` · `**för senare.**`
9. `**boka**` · `en genomgång`
10. `säg till` · `så bygger jag` · `**din.**`
11. `skriv` · `**"ja"**` · `så kör vi`
12. `vill du se` · `hela **flödet?**`
13. `**länk**` · `i första` · `kommentaren`
14. `redo att` · `**börja?**`
15. `ta det här` · `till **ditt** team`
16. `en fråga` · `släng den` · `**nedan.**`

## Pairing note

Pairs with `cta-scene`: the coral highlight on the CTA keyword should mirror the scene's single
coral pill button — coral fires *once* across the plate, so if the button is the coral instance,
keep the caption highlight to the same word family (`kommentera` / `länken`). One ask only. Don't
stack two CTAs in one caption run. End on a full-stop chunk so the short closes clean.
