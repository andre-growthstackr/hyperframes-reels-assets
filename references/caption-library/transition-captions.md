# transition-captions

**What is this?** Short caption chunks for the seam *between* sections — the connective tissue that
carries the viewer from one beat to the next. Usually 1–2 words, often the pivot in a denial-pivot.

**When to use it?** Across a `light-flare-transition` (the soft coral light-leak between major
sections). The caption chunk swaps as the flare passes, so the new section lands with a word already
on screen. Not every transition needs a caption — use sparingly, at real section breaks.

**What feeling?** A clean breath. Calm, digital, momentum without noise. The pivot word carries it
(*men*, *nu*, *sen*, *därför*). No drum-roll, no "but wait".

---

Format per line: chunks `[…]`, coral highlight in **bold** where a pivot word earns it.

1. `**men** sen`
2. `så här` · `**istället.**`
3. `**nu** då?`
4. `här är` · `**varför.**`
5. `steg` · `**två.**`
6. `och` · `**sen** detta`
7. `**därför.**`
8. `nästa` · `**grej.**`
9. `kolla` · `**detta.**`
10. `en sak` · `**till.**`
11. `**men.**`
12. `så` · `**vad** hände?`
13. `resultatet` · `**?**`
14. `vänta` · `på **det** här`

## Pairing note

Pairs with `light-flare-transition`: drop the caption chunk so it *starts* just before the flare
peaks and *swaps* as the bloom drifts L→R — the word bridges the two sections. Reserve for the 3–6
real transition points in a 30–60s short, synced to the swoosh SFX. Keep these to one or two words:
a transition caption that runs long stops feeling like a transition. Pivot words (`men`, `nu`, `sen`,
`därför`) are the denial-pivot hinge — coral them once.
