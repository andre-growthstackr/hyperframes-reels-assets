# motion-references/lower-thirds/

**Built component:** none dedicated — composed from
[`../../../components/caption-pill`](../../../components/caption-pill) styling + a name/label strip
per `frame.md`. (The caption-pill is the library's signature caption; a lower-third borrows its dark
charcoal pill + bold white Inter look for a name / role / handle strip.)

## What is this?
Clips of **lower-third / label strips** you like — a name, role, or handle animating in near the
lower band and out again. The reference feel for the on-brand identifier strip.

## When to use it?
Pull when adding a speaker name, a handle, or a small contextual label to a talking-head or proof
beat. Match the in/out timing and the resting position to the clips here. Note: the *spoken-word*
caption is always the `caption-pill` at the seam — a lower-third is a separate, quieter identifier,
never a competing subtitle.

## What feeling?
Quiet, editorial, calm. It informs without stealing the frame; it never bottom-sticks like a
YouTube subtitle.

### What I like (target bullets)
- Slides / fades in fast, settles slow, no bounce; exits clean.
- Dark charcoal pill, bold white Inter — borrows the caption-pill look for consistency.
- At most one coral atom (a handle, a dot), never a coral bar.
- Sits above the true bottom edge, out of the safe margin — never bottom-stuck.
- Short dwell — in, read, out; it doesn't linger the whole scene.
- Doesn't collide with the seam caption-pill; different band, different job.

Drop clips + a `notes.md` (shape in the parent README). `.gitkeep` holds the folder until then.
